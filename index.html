Cardiology

Medications in cardiology focus on treating conditions related to the heart and blood vessels

This includes drugs for managing hypertension (high blood pressure), angina (chest pain), arrhythmias (irregular heart rhythms), heart failure, and reducing the risk of heart attacks and strokes. Common medications include beta-blockers, ACE inhibitors, calcium channel blockers, and antiplatelet drugs.

Neurology

Neurological medications treat disorders of the nervous system, which includes the brain, spinal cord, and nerves

This category covers treatments for epilepsy (anticonvulsants), Parkinson's disease (dopamine agonists, MAO-B inhibitors), multiple sclerosis (immunomodulators), Alzheimer's disease (cholinesterase inhibitors, NMDA receptor antagonists), and migraine headaches (triptans, beta-blockers)

Hepatology

Hepatology medications are used to treat liver diseases such as hepatitis (antivirals), cirrhosis (diuretics, beta-blockers), liver cancer (chemotherapy agents, targeted therapies), and fatty liver disease (lifestyle modifications, insulin sensitizers

These medications aim to manage symptoms, slow disease progression, and improve liver function.

Pediatrics

Pediatric medications are specially formulated and dosed for children to treat common childhood illnesses and conditions

This includes antibiotics for infections, bronchodilators for asthma, antihistamines for allergies, vaccines for preventive care, and medications for attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) and other developmental disorders.

Eye Care

Medications in eye care address various conditions affecting the eyes.

This includes treatments for glaucoma (prostaglandin analogs, beta-blockers), dry eyes (artificial tears, cyclosporine), allergies (antihistamines, mast cell stabilizers), infections (antibiotics), and cataracts (anti-inflammatory agents). Eye care medications aim to improve vision, reduce discomfort, and prevent vision loss.
